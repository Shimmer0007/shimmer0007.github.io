The "Win Rate ≠ Popularity" Paradox

Key Findings from Core Metrics

Correlation Analysis

Statistical indicators ($R^2 \approx 0.11$) reveal a weak correlation between Win Rate and Pick Rate. Most heroes cluster between 45%-55% Win Rate, yet popularity varies wildly (0-10%).

The "Niche" Effect

Heroes like Symmetra & Torbjörn maintain high win rates (>54%) but remain unpopular. They are "specialists" used only in specific scenarios, not universally strong picks.

2. Trend Depth Analysis (Kyle)

VISUALIZATION

Role Dynamics & Structural Imbalance

Key Findings from Trend Analysis

  • Structural Skew

    While Win Rates are stable, Pick Rates are skewed due to limited Tank/Support options. This forces higher pick concentration compared to Damage heroes.

  • Tier Specificity

    Performance is highly skill-dependent (e.g., Moira falls off in high tiers). "One-size-fits-all" balancing is ineffective; adjustments must be tier-specific.

  • Critical Anomaly

    Lifeweaver (39.8%) is a severe negative outlier, requiring immediate intervention beyond simple number tweaks.

VISUALIZATION

Mechanisms vs. Metrics

Why High Stats Don't Mean High Wins

High Output ≠ High Win Rate

Mercy & Kiriko have massive raw healing numbers but average win rates. Their value lies in utility (Rez, Suzu), not just the HPS/DPS numbers shown on the scoreboard.

Role Identity Crisis

Objective Time data shows Supports (71.7s) overlapping heavily with Tanks (98.6s). This forces Supports into risky positioning, negatively impacting their survival and win probability.

Coefficient Analysis: Anomalies & Drivers

KDA Ratio: -0.718 PARADOX

The negative coefficient implies higher KDA correlates with lower pick rates. This confirms the Specialist Effect: Niche heroes (e.g., Symmetra) achieve inflated stats, but lack general popularity.

Deaths/10m: -1.010

A strong negative relationship. For every death increase, the pick rate receives a severe penalty. This suggests Survival is a prerequisite for popularity.

DPS+Healing: +0.0005

This term is the single most significant predictor ($P < 8E^{-40}$). Raw output is a strong primary signal, but its linearity limits its explanatory power to only 35%.

Support: Utility Focused

Highest Predictability (R² ≈ 0.40)

  • CRITICAL FINDING: Total Output / 10m is NOT a significant predictor.
  • Logic: Players prioritize Functionality (e.g., Ana's anti-heal, Kiriko's Suzu) over raw HPS/DPS numbers.
  • Strongest Penalty: Deaths / 10min. Survival is the most critical metric for popularity.
  • Significant Predictors: Win Rate, KDA Ratio, Deaths.

Damage: Stat Driven

Medium Predictability (R² ≈ 0.32)

  • Key Insight: This is the only role where standard panel metrics are *ALL* significant predictors.
  • Logic: Damage heroes follow the most conventional "Carry" logic; high stats (KDA, Total Output, Obj Kills) equate to high pick rates.
  • Significant Predictors: Win Rate, KDA Ratio, Total Output, Deaths, Objective Kills.
  • Anomaly: Objective Kills Coefficient is negative, suggesting players prefer flanking/off-site kills over point-brawling.

Tank: Contextual Pick

Lowest Predictability (R² ≈ 0.23)

  • Logic: Low R² indicates Non-Data Factors (Map geometry, team synergy, counter-picking) are the primary drivers.
  • Conclusion: Tank pick rates are driven by the meta context ("Is the enemy team playing Zarya?") not just raw hero performance numbers.
  • Significant Predictors: All 5 metrics are technically significant, but their collective explanatory power is weak.
Overall Conclusion: Separating roles was essential. The Global Model's anomalies were artifacts of mixing the "Utility" (Support), "Stat" (Damage), and "Context" (Tank) heroes.

Model 3: GLM (Gamma + Log Link)

The "Final Truth": Modeling Non-Linear Popularity (Pseudo R² ≈ 0.52)

0.52

Pseudo R²

Gamma Family

Addresses the right-skewed Pick Rate (Long Tail).

Log Link

Enables exponential interpretation: $\text{Pick Rate} \propto e^{\text{Coeff}}$.

Features

Includes WinRate⁴, Survival Factor, and Tier Dummies.

Core Drivers of Pick Rate:

$$ \begin{aligned} \ln(\text{Pick Rate}) = \ & -1.30 \\ & + \mathbf{7.03E^{-8}} \times (\text{Win Rate}^4) \quad \leftarrow \text{Meta Strength} \\ & + \mathbf{0.125} \times \text{Survival Factor} \quad \leftarrow \text{Crucial for Popularity} \\ & + 0.0001 \times \text{Total Impact} \\ & - 0.0073 \times \text{Carry Score} \\ & - 0.0044 \times \text{Chore Duty} \\ & + 0.559 \times \text{Tier}_{\text{GM}} \end{aligned} $$

Equation scaled for clarity: Coefficients indicate relative impact on log-transformed pick rate.

Key Coefficient Interpretations

Survival Factor: +0.125

Interpretation: Every 1-unit increase in survival boosts pick rate by $\mathbf{e^{0.125} \approx 13.3\%}$.

Players prioritize longevity over raw output, confirming the "survivability premium" in hero selection.

WinRate⁴: Exponential

Interpretation: The 4th-power transformation captures community's exponential response to perceived strength.

Small win rate advantages become massively amplified in popularity through meta perception.

GM Tier Coeff: +0.559

Interpretation: The highest tier coefficient demonstrates that top players are ruthless optimizers.

Grandmaster players amplify meta effects by ~75% ($e^{0.559} \approx 1.75\times$) compared to average tiers.

Chore Duty: -0.0044

Interpretation: Objective Time ("Chore Duty") remains a significant negative predictor.

Players systematically avoid heroes associated with point duties, preferring high-mobility or flanking roles.

Model Summary

  • Pseudo R² = 0.52 – The non-linear GLM explains 52% of pick rate variance, a 48% improvement over the global OLS model.
  • Non-linearity confirmed – The WinRate⁴ term validates our hypothesis that popularity follows an exponential response to perceived strength.
  • Tier stratification essential – The significant GM coefficient proves balancing must consider skill brackets separately.
